Jeremiah Jones, PhD

Advisor - Statistics Eli Lilly and Company
Data and decision scientist, singer, tinkerer, and lover of dogs.

I’m currently an Advisor in Statistics at Eli Lilly and Company, where I work on generating evidence for efficacy and safety of promising new drugs.

Prior to starting at Lilly, I was a PhD Candidate and Graduate Research Assistant at the University of Rochester, where I studied under Drs. Ashkan Ertefaie and Robert L. Strawderman.

My statistical research focuses on the intersection of causal inference with high dimensionality, selection, and nonregular inference problems. Some of my interests are:

  • Robust marginal models for efficacy and dose-response
  • Causal mediation analysis
  • Variable selection methods in causal Inference settings
  • Post-selection inference techniques
  • Personalized medicine through dynamic treatment regimes
